## Key Concepts and Definitions  

- **Machine Learning (ML)** – A subset of artificial intelligence (AI) that enables computers to learn patterns from data and make predictions or decisions without explicit programming【1】.  
- **Core ML Techniques in Education** – Decision trees, random forests, support‑vector machines, neural networks (including deep learning), clustering, collaborative filtering, and reinforcement learning【2】.  
- **Adaptive Learning** – Systems that adjust instructional content in real time based on a learner’s knowledge state, often using **Bayesian Knowledge Tracing** (a probabilistic model of mastery) or reinforcement learning【3】.  
- **Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITS)** – AI‑driven platforms that provide personalized tutoring, feedback, and hints, mimicking one‑on‑one instruction【4】.  
- **Learning Analytics & Predictive Modeling** – Use of ML to analyze interaction data, predict outcomes (e.g., dropout risk), and inform early‑intervention strategies【5】.  
- **Natural Language Processing (NLP) & Generative AI** – Large language models (LLMs) such as GPT‑4, Claude, and Gemini used for auto‑generated quizzes, essay feedback, and conversational tutoring【6】.
